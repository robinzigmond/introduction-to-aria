(Very) basics of ARIA

Note: ARIA is a complicated spec. Each role has its own rules regarding how it should behave. It's hard to get everything absolutely right - which is one reason not to use it unless absolutely necessary!

But what ARIA does, in simple terms, is allow you to directly specify the properties needed to determine what the accessibility tree looks like:

  • The 
    role
    attribute gives the element's role
  • various attributes of the form 
    aria-XXX
    provide the state and property values
  • the 
    aria-label
    or 
    aria-labelledby
    attributes can be used to provide an accessible name if necessary
